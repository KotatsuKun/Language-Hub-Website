Dutch Language

Exploring the history and cultural significance of the Dutch language.

History of Dutch

Dutch evolved from Old Frankish and became distinct from other Germanic languages around the 5th century. The standardization of Dutch began in the 16th century, with the Statenvertaling (State Translation) of the Bible playing a key role.

Cultural Significance

Dutch is the language of famous painters like Rembrandt and Van Gogh, and philosophers like Spinoza. It's also the ancestor of Afrikaans, spoken in South Africa and Namibia, and has influenced many creole languages.

Learning Resources

Dutch is considered one of the easiest languages for English speakers to learn due to its similar vocabulary and grammar. However, its pronunciation, particularly the guttural 'g' sound, can be challenging for beginners.
