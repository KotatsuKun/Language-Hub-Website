Russian Language

Exploring the rich history and cultural significance of the Russian language.

History of Russian

Russian is an East Slavic language that evolved from Old East Slavic, the language of Kievan Rus'. It was standardized in the 18th century during the reign of Peter the Great, with Mikhail Lomonosov and Alexander Pushkin playing key roles in its development.

Cultural Significance

Russian is the language of literary giants like Tolstoy, Dostoevsky, and Chekhov. It has a rich tradition in ballet, classical music, and science, and served as the lingua franca of the Soviet Union and much of the Eastern Bloc.

Learning Resources

Russian is known for its complex grammar, including six cases and aspects of verbs. The Cyrillic alphabet, while initially challenging, is phonetic and can be learned relatively quickly. Many learners find the language's logical structure rewarding.
